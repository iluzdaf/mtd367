# Lesson 01 Slides

Canva is the source of truth for these slides.

## Focus

Introduce the course structure, assessment rhythm, reasons for focusing on native iOS development, the Xcode environment, and the minimum Swift syntax students need before building app interfaces.

## Learning Outcomes

- Explain the course workflow, lesson structure, practical session purpose, and assessment weighting.
- Describe why Swift and native iOS development are useful foundations for mobile app work.
- Navigate Xcode at a basic level, including the editor, navigator, and console.
- Use Swift variables, constants, basic types, operators, functions, conditionals, and loops.
- Read, run, and modify small Swift examples in a Playground.

## Rough Deck Flow

1. Course welcome and expectations.
2. Course learning objectives and lesson timeline.
3. Practical sessions and assessment breakdown.
4. Why Swift, why native iOS, and why local context matters.
5. Lesson 1 foundations and Xcode orientation.
6. Swift values, types, operators, and type safety.
7. Functions, decisions, repetition, and small problem-solving patterns.
8. Hands-on practice with short runnable exercises.
9. Summary and practical-session follow-up.

## Demo And Practice Direction

Use compact Swift Playground examples that students can modify quickly. Start with `print("Hello, world!")`, then move into small examples for `let`/`var`, basic types, operators, functions, conditionals, and loops. Practice should emphasize reading code, predicting output, and making small changes rather than building a full app yet.

## Assessment Preparation

This lesson builds core Swift confidence for later public practice, TMA readiness, and ECA preparation. Assessment discussion should stay high level: TMAs as stepping stones, the ECA as synthesis, and the importance of code, planning, design reasoning, and explanation. Do not include private assessment questions, rubrics, marking guides, or model answers.
